The Thesis

Not random. Inevitable.

This is not a random acquisition. These two companies are converging on the same destination from opposite directions.

WEFUN started with employee welfare and expanded into office operations. Lyreco started with office supplies and expanded into workplace services. They are building the same thing — a total workplace platform — from different starting points, in different markets, with complementary strengths.

The Cross-Sell Thesis

Same buyer. Same desk.

"The procurement decision-maker — the 총무팀 (General Affairs team) — is identical for both companies' products. One invoice. One platform. Everything the office needs."
The Strategic Logic

This is the insight that makes the deal work. WEFUN already sells snacks, coffee, and corporate gifts to the same office managers who buy supplies, furniture, and PPE from Lyreco. The customer overlap is not coincidental — it's structural. The 총무팀 manages the entire workplace. Combining both catalogs under one platform eliminates procurement friction and dramatically increases wallet share per client.

The Combined Entity

What convergence unlocks

🔀
Cross-Sell Acceleration
WEFUN's 10,000 clients get access to Lyreco's full catalog. Lyreco's MNC clients discover WEFUN's welfare platform. Current cross-sell ratio: 18% — target: 50%. The upside is enormous and immediate.
🌏
Asia-Wide Platform
WEFUN services deployed across 4 additional Asian markets overnight. Korea becomes the proof of concept; Thailand, Malaysia, Singapore, and Australia become the growth story. This transforms the IPO narrative from "Korean platform" to "Asia-wide ecosystem."
🏛️
MNC Access
Lyreco's global corporate clients — multinationals with long-tenure, high-ARPU accounts managed through global procurement agreements. These are relationships that take decades to build. WEFUN gets instant access to enterprise-grade revenue streams.
📊
Valuation Benchmark
Kokuyo acquired 65% of Vietnam's Thien Long Group for ~$185M at ~2.4x revenue (Dec 2025). Japanese office products giant buying into Asian distribution — the exact same thesis, from a comparable buyer profile.
