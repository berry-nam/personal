Asia Zone

Hong Kong is the nerve center

Lyreco's Asia Zone headquarters is in Hong Kong. The zone is led by Jones Leung, who joined Lyreco in 2019 as Managing Director of Hong Kong and was subsequently promoted to Zone Managing Director for all of Asia.

Leung holds a degree from the Chinese University of Hong Kong and brings both B2C and B2B experience to the role. He is active on LinkedIn, regularly posting about wellness showcases, employee experience awards, and corporate culture initiatives — a signal that Lyreco Asia is leaning heavily into the same workplace wellness positioning that defines WEFUN.

Understanding who runs the zone matters. If this deal proceeds, Jones Leung and his team are the people WEFUN would inherit. Their culture, their client relationships, their operational knowledge — all of it transfers.

The Architect

Gregory Lienard's Legacy

The current CEO, Gregory Lienard, personally built Lyreco's Asian operations. Before ascending to the top role in 2019, Lienard was posted across Canada, France, and Singapore — each assignment deepening his understanding of how to operate across cultures and markets.

His Asian expansion strategy followed a deliberate sequence: Hong Kong as the beachhead, Korea as the second anchor, then Singapore via the Deskright acquisition in 2019, which served as the launchpad for Malaysia and Thailand.

This matters because when WEFUN approaches Lyreco about the Asian operations, you are approaching something the CEO built with his own hands. Lienard has personal attachment to these markets. The conversation must honor that — and demonstrate that WEFUN will grow what he planted, not uproot it.

The Mirror

The Korea Operation
— A Mirror

This is where the story becomes extraordinary. Lyreco Korea operates a brand called "Lounge Service" (lyrecoloungeservice.com) — a B2B total wellness and welfare platform that directly mirrors WEFUN's core offering.

Food & Snacks Office Operations Safety Equipment Corporate Gifting Workplace Wellness

The overlap is stunning. Lounge Service offers food and snack subscriptions, office operations support, safety products, and corporate gifting — the exact same service categories that define WEFUN's platform. And Lyreco Korea is headquartered in Gangnam-gu, Seoul (Teheran-ro) — literally the same neighborhood as WEFUN.

This isn't coincidence — it's convergence. Two companies, operating in the same city, in the same neighborhood, serving the same customer need, arrived at the same product-market conclusion independently. One from Europe moving east, one from Korea expanding outward. They are mirror images.

Lounge Service = WEFUN's Proof of Concept

The fact that Lyreco independently developed a welfare/wellness B2B offering in Korea validates WEFUN's entire thesis. If a century-old European distributor reached the same conclusion about what Korean offices need, then WEFUN's platform isn't just a local trend — it's a universal insight about the future of workplace services.

Same Neighborhood, Different Scale

Lyreco Korea in Gangnam-gu generates ~₩26.87B. WEFUN, also rooted in Gangnam/Seoul, generates ₩143.1B and growing at 87% YoY. The acquisition would combine local knowledge with platform velocity. Lyreco Korea's client relationships plus WEFUN's technology and operational machine creates immediate synergy.

Integration, Not Disruption

Because the product offerings already overlap, integration becomes natural rather than forced. WEFUN wouldn't be imposing a foreign model on Lyreco Korea's clients — it would be enhancing an existing service with deeper technology, better logistics, and a broader product catalog. The Lounge Service brand could evolve seamlessly into the WEFUN platform.

"The Korea and Singapore operations are embryonic versions of what WEFUN has already perfected. This isn't just a distribution acquisition — it's a market convergence."
Strategic Assessment
Key Insight

Small today. Strategic forever.

The Asian operations are small relative to the Lyreco group — less than 10% of total revenue. For a €2.2 billion enterprise focused on its European heartland and approaching its centenary, Asia may feel like a distraction. The operations require attention, investment, and local expertise that a French headquarters 9,000 kilometers away struggles to provide effectively.

But for WEFUN, these same operations are transformational. Five established markets with existing client relationships, proven infrastructure, and regulatory compliance already in place. Under WEFUN's stewardship, with the full platform toolkit — Snack24, Coffee24, Lunch24, Gift24, the logistics backbone, the technology stack — these embryonic operations could grow dramatically.

What is peripheral to Lyreco is central to WEFUN. That asymmetry is what makes this deal logical for both sides. Lyreco focuses on its European centenary strength. WEFUN builds the Asian workplace solutions platform that Lyreco started but couldn't complete from 9,000 kilometers away.

The narrative for Lyreco

Your Asian operations deserve a partner who lives in the market, speaks the language, and has already built the platform you were trying to build. We're not acquiring distressed assets — we're completing the vision you started.
