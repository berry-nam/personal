Understanding Lyreco's origins is essential for the February 26 meeting. When you sit across from Eric Bigeard and Helen Riley, you're sitting across from the living memory of this story.

From Chapter IV — Lyreco Goes East
Acquisition Thesis & Timing

The asymmetry that makes this deal work

Lounge Service = WEFUN's Proof of Concept

The fact that Lyreco independently developed a welfare/wellness B2B offering in Korea validates WEFUN's entire thesis. They built "Lounge Service" — a workplace wellness product — without knowing that WEFUN had already built the platform they were trying to build.

"What is peripheral to Lyreco is central to WEFUN. That asymmetry is what makes this deal logical for both sides."
Core Thesis

Pitch Framing

"Your Asian operations deserve a partner who lives in the market, speaks the language, and has already built the platform you were trying to build. We're not acquiring distressed assets — we're completing the vision you started."

Peripheral vs. Central

Asia is a non-core region for Lyreco's European-focused strategy. For WEFUN, it's the entire addressable market. That mismatch creates value for both sides.

Validation, Not Speculation

Lyreco already invested in building a welfare product in Korea. WEFUN isn't pitching a theory — it's pointing to Lyreco's own experiments as proof of convergence.

Completing the Vision

Frame the acquisition as continuation, not disruption. WEFUN picks up where Lyreco's Asian ambitions left off — with a platform that's already at scale.

The governing principles

Across every chapter, the same strategic principles emerge repeatedly. These are the non-negotiable foundations of WEFUN's approach to the Lyreco Asia acquisition.

Respect the Legacy
You are asking a 100-year-old family to trust you with a piece of their inheritance. Every interaction must acknowledge the emotional weight of what Lyreco represents to the Gaspards.
Win Bigeard First
His approval is the prerequisite. He is evaluating character, not spreadsheets. Demonstrate sophistication, humility, and genuine respect for what he built.

What is peripheral to Lyreco is central to WEFUN. Frame the deal as mutual benefit — not as WEFUN extracting value from a distressed seller.
Culture as Proof
WEFUN's employee satisfaction data and acquisition track record are the evidence that Lyreco's 700 Asian employees will be treated with respect.

You are completing the vision Lyreco started. The pitch is stewardship and acceleration — not takeover and restructuring.
The IPO Transformation
This deal transforms WEFUN's narrative from "Korean platform" to "Asia-wide ecosystem." The cross-sell thesis (18% to 50%) is the financial engine. The story is the multiplier.
The Bottom Line
This is not a financial transaction.
This is an act of trust.

Every piece of strategic intelligence in this playbook points to the same conclusion: the Lyreco Asia acquisition will be won or lost on trust, not on price. The Gaspard family, Eric Bigeard, and Helen Riley must believe — viscerally, not intellectually — that WEFUN is the right steward for what they built.
