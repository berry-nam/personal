Beyond Lyreco

The family's investment vehicles

The Gaspard family's interests extend well beyond office supplies. Understanding their broader investment activity reveals the sophistication and ambition of this dynasty.

Corely Belgium SPRL & Bratya SPRL

Belgian investment vehicles managed by David Coti since 2015. Coti is an ESSEC and Plekhanov University graduate — signaling the family hires world-class talent to manage its non-Lyreco assets. These entities invested €15 million + €3.6 million in CARMAT — the French artificial heart company. This is not a family that plays it safe. They back visionary, high-risk ventures alongside their steady B2B cash cow.

Georges Gaspard — Extended Network

Beyond the formal holding structure, Georges Gaspard is connected to multiple entities: Erypharm SAS Nataline SC Villa Geoanne du Cap Martin — the latter being a real estate entity based in Onnaing. This web of interests paints a picture of a patriarch who is deeply engaged across industries, not merely a figurehead atop a single company.

The Auditors

PwC on the books.
Institutional governance.

Lyreco's statutory auditors are PricewaterhouseCoopers (previously KPMG). For a family-owned company with no stock market listing, choosing a Big Four auditor is a deliberate signal. It tells banks, partners, and potential counterparties: we run this like a public company, even though we don't have to.

This matters for the deal. It means Lyreco's financials will be clean, well-documented, and prepared to institutional standards. Due diligence will be rigorous — but the data will be there.

The Meaning

This is not a public corporation

"This is not a public corporation optimizing quarterly earnings. This is a family's century-long legacy. Every decision is measured not in fiscal quarters, but in generations."
Key Insight

The Gaspards don't just own Lyreco. They are Lyreco. The company was founded by their ancestor, expanded by their patriarch, and is now being stewarded by the fourth generation. When you sit across the table from a Lyreco representative, you are not dealing with hired management accountable to anonymous shareholders. You are dealing with a family that has invested a hundred years of its name, its reputation, and its capital into this enterprise.

This is why the approach to any acquisition must be fundamentally different from a typical corporate transaction. Price matters — but it is not enough. The Gaspards need to believe that the buyer will honor the culture, protect the employees, and continue the legacy. A higher bid from a private equity firm that plans to strip and flip would lose to a lower bid from someone the family trusts.

What This Means for the Deal

The family structure is your biggest obstacle and your biggest opportunity. It is an obstacle because the Gaspards will never sell to someone they do not trust — no matter the price. It is an opportunity because if you earn that trust, the deal dynamics shift entirely. The Gaspards want a partner who thinks in decades, not quarters. WEFUN's founding story — built by entrepreneurs, not financial engineers — is exactly the kind of narrative that resonates with a family dynasty. Lead with that.
