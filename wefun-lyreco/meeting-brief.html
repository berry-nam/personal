The Premise

This is not a negotiation.
This is a courtship.

On February 26, two management teams will meet for the first time. One represents a 7-year-old Korean B2B platform growing at 87% annually. The other represents a French family business celebrating its 100th anniversary, whose board member personally grew the company from €15M to €2B.

Chapter I

The Buyer — WEFUN

Founded on Christmas Eve 2018 by Kim Heon, WEFUN began with a simple premise: workplaces in Korea were broken, and someone needed to fix them — one snack box at a time. What started as Snack24, a subscription snack delivery for offices, has evolved into Korea's dominant B2B workplace-as-a-service platform.

Today, WEFUN operates roughly 100 modular services across four pillars: employee welfare, corporate gifting SaaS, office operations management, and space management. Over 10,000 B2B clients and 800,000 employee touchpoints trust WEFUN daily. Their client retention rate: 97%.

Chapter II

The Seller — Lyreco

In 1926, in Valenciennes, a small town in northern France, Georges-Gaston Gaspard opened a bookshop. From that modest storefront, four generations of the Gaspard family built Europe's largest B2B workplace products distributor — a ~€3 billion enterprise operating across 25 countries with 10,000+ employees.

This year, 2026, Lyreco celebrates its 100th anniversary. The family controls Lyreco through Corely SAS → Holding Lyreco France SAS → Lyreco SAS. The ~84% figure dates from the 2008 Corporate Express deal; the exact current percentage is unverifiable since France restricted beneficial-ownership access in July 2024. Nathalie Gaspard serves as President. This is not a public corporation optimizing quarterly earnings. This is a family's century-long legacy.

No public announcement has been made, but the signals are unmistakable. All recent acquisitions (2019–2024) are European. Helen Riley's newly created role explicitly includes "divestments." A massive executive team refresh in 2025 — new CFO, Chief Growth Officer, Chief People Officer — signals strategic reset. Precedent exists: Lyreco sold its Australian operations to COS in 2018.

What's Being Acquired

Direct operations in five markets: Korea, Hong Kong (zone HQ), Singapore, Malaysia, Thailand. 655 FTE. Lyreco Korea alone generates €16M (2025) from their Gangnam headquarters. Their product catalog spans office supplies, IT products, furniture, PPE, hygiene, and — crucially — workplace food, snack, coffee, and lounge services that directly mirror WEFUN's core offerings.

The "Lounge Service" brand in Korea and "Wellness@Pantry" subscription service in Singapore/Malaysia are embryonic versions of what WEFUN has already perfected. This isn't just a distribution acquisition — it's a market convergence.

The Meeting Playbook

This is 70% relationship, 30% substance. The meeting architecture should feel like a conversation between two companies who share a belief, not a pitch deck review.

Presentation Flow

5 minutes
Welcome & Acknowledgment
Recognize Lyreco's 100th anniversary. Honor the Gaspard family's four-generation legacy. Express what it means to host their management team at WEFUN.
20 minutes
WEFUN's Story
The founding narrative, growth journey, vision of "making offices a joy to work in." Service portfolio, client base, culture. Lead with story, not slides.

Bolt-on strategy. Integration approach. Employee retention in past deals. The Human Food PE-partnership model. Show you know how to honor what you acquire.

"Total workplace platform" thesis. Cross-sell opportunity. Asia expansion potential. How Lyreco's Asian operations will flourish with new capabilities.

30+ minutes

The most important segment. Answer Lyreco's questions with transparency. Build rapport. Discuss next steps. Let them lead the conversation.

Five Things to Avoid

⛔ No valuation talk

Don't discuss specific deal terms or pricing. This is a relationship meeting, not a negotiation session.

⛔ No "synergies through cost-cutting"

This translates to "layoffs" in a family seller's mind. Frame everything as growth investment, not extraction.

⛔ Don't rush

French family businesses move deliberately. Patience signals respect. Let silences breathe.

⛔ No vague promises on employees

Make concrete, specific commitments. Ideally: guarantee all employees for 18–24 months, preserve compensation and local management.

⛔ Don't underestimate the emotion

For Lyreco, this is about a family legacy — not just a P&L line item. Every word you say about the Asian team will be weighed.
